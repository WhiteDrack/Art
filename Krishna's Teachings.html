Shri Krishna's Essential Teachings from Bhagavad Gita

"This life is like a Dharmakshetra or Kurukshetra" - where man should perform his duties and not just do Puja-Paath or Bhajan.

"The human body is also a Kurukshetra" - where divine and demonic resolves are at war every moment.

"The war of Mahabharata was a real historical event" - which was used by Ved-Vyas to show man the true form of Dharma.

"Shri Krishna asked Arjuna to stand the chariot between the two armies" - so that Arjuna could see who all were involved in the war.

Arjuna was filled with great compassion on seeing his relatives in the war and decided not to fight.

Arjuna's body started trembling, his face became dry, the Gandiva bow started slipping from his hand, and his mind started getting confused.

Arjuna said:

  • He did not desire victory, kingdom or happiness
  • Those for whom he wanted all this were all standing ready to sacrifice their lives and wealth in the war
  • Seeing his Gurus, fathers, sons, grandfathers, uncles, fathers-in-law, grandsons and friends present in the war, Arjuna did not wish to kill them
  • Even for the kingdom of Trilok he did not wish to kill them, so how could he kill them for the earth
  • We will not get any happiness by killing the Kauravas, because by killing these tyrants we will incur sins

"न काङ्क्षे विजयं कृष्ण न च राज्यं सुखानि च"
"I desire not victory, O Krishna, nor kingdom, nor pleasures" (Bhagavad Gita 1.31)
