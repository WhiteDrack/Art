Shri Krishna's Essential Teachings from Bhagavad Gita

"This life is Dharmakshetra-Kurukshetra" - The battlefield of righteousness where we must fulfill our duties beyond mere rituals.

"कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन"
"You have the right to perform your duties, but not to the fruits of action" (Bhagavad Gita 2.47)

"The human body is Kurukshetra" - An eternal battlefield where divine and demonic forces within us wage constant war.

"Mahabharata as Dharma's Mirror" - Ved Vyas ji documented this historical war to reveal Dharma's true nature to mankind.

Arjuna's Crisis on the Battlefield

When Shri Krishna positioned their chariot between the armies, Arjuna saw:

  • His gurus (Dronacharya, Kripa Acharya)
  • Elders (Bhishma Pitamah)
  • Cousins (Kauravas)
  • Friends and relatives

Arjuna's physical and emotional turmoil:

  • His body trembled violently
  • Face became pale and dry
  • Gandiva bow slipped from his grasp
  • Mind became utterly confused

"दृष्ट्वेमं स्वजनं कृष्ण युयुत्सुं समुपस्थितम्"
"Seeing these my kinsmen, O Krishna, arrayed for battle, my limbs fail me" (Bhagavad Gita 1.28)

Arjuna's heartfelt confession to Krishna:

  • "I desire neither victory nor kingdom nor pleasures"
  • "What value is kingship when those we fight for stand ready to die?"
  • "Not for the three worlds would I kill my gurus and elders"
  • "Slaying these tyrants will only bring us sin, not happiness"

"न काङ्क्षे विजयं कृष्ण न च राज्यं सुखानि च"
"I desire not victory, O Krishna, nor kingdom, nor pleasures" (Bhagavad Gita 1.31)
